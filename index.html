Outlander

Created by Diana Gabaldon

The Outlander franchise consists of a core book series, related novellas and comics, and a Starz television adaptation. At its heart, its main character is Claire Randall, a 20th century English nurse. At the seriesâ€™s start, Claire and her husband Frank are on their second honeymoon in Scotland, celebrating their reunion now that World War II has ended, and looking forward to settling into a life together. Mysteriously, however, she seems to stumble through a hole in time and finds herself in Scotland 200 years earlier, at the height of Highlander culture. In trying to navigate her surroundings and stay alive in a land not friendly to English citizens, she is torn between two husbands - one in 1946 and one in 1743, on the precipice of an attempted revolution that would end Highland life for the people she has come to love.

About the author:

Diana Gabaldon is from Arizona, and had never been to Scotland before beginning to write about it. She has a B.S. in Zoology, a M.S. in Marine Biology, and a Ph.D. in Quantitative Behavioral Ecology, and was a university professor with an expertise in scientific computation before she became a writer. She is a Co-Producer and advisor for the Starz network television adaptation of Outlander.
