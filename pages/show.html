Outlander

Created by Diana Gabaldon

The Show

Outlander has been adapted into a television series on Starz, which began filming in the fall of 2013 and began airing on August 9, 2014. Each season follows the plot of its corresponding novel. In May 2018, the series was renewed for fifth and sixth seasons, which will cover the plots of A Fiery Cross and A Breath of Snow and Ashes respectively.

The theme song is an adaptation of Robert Louis Stevenson's poem Sing me a Song of a Lad that is Gone, set to the tune of the Scottish folk song "The Skye Boat Song", which alludes to Bonnie Prince Charlie escaping by boat after his defeat at Culloden during the last Jacobite rising in 1746. As locales and themes change throughout the different series, the language and instruments of the song are adapted to reflect the new environments of the show. This is the theme from the first season, which is focused entirely on Scotland, and therefore prominently features traditional Scottish instruments like the bagpipe.
